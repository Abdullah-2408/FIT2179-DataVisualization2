Section 1 · The Big Picture

Food in Malaysia has never been more expensive

The Food Consumer Price Index (CPI) measures how much the price of a typical basket of food has changed over time. A value of 100 represents the baseline year.

Since January 2019, Malaysia's food CPI has climbed from 131 to over 155 — an increase of more than 18%.

The steepest rise happened in 2022, driven by global supply chain disruptions, surging commodity prices, and a weakening ringgit.

While the pace of increase has since slowed, prices show no sign of returning to pre-pandemic levels. Malaysians are now permanently paying more for the same basket of food.

Section 2 · The Geography of Cost

Not every state pays the same price

National averages hide a striking divide. Food costs vary dramatically across Malaysia's 16 states — by as much as 28% between the cheapest and most expensive regions.

The federal territories of Putrajaya, Kuala Lumpur, and the industrialised states of Selangor and Johor sit at the top of the price ladder.

Meanwhile, East Malaysia (Sabah, Labuan) and the northern states (Perlis, Kedah) enjoy noticeably lower food costs.
